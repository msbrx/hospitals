Hospital Access disproportionately higher in more affluent NY boroughs

By Moses Bustos
December 17th, 2020

Covid-19 hospitalization rates are the highest they’ve been since June. NYC hospitals struggle to stay operational as medical staff continue to dwindle and patient capacity reaches critical levels. This drastic need for health infrastructure highlights the disparity in healthcare access across the boroughs.

Manhattan, with a population of approximately 1.6 million, boasts more than double the hospitals in the Bronx which is home to approximately 1.4 million people. Dr. Rayna Sobieski, 32, Family Medicine Attending at The Institute for Family Health in the Bronx, says “while H+H is a great source of pride for NYC and serves a large portion of the uninsured population, it is severely underfunded and wait times can be long.

Patients with private insurance can access medical care much more swiftly outside of the Bronx.” 3 out of 13 hospitals in the city are part of H+H compared to 3 of 6 hospitals in the Bronx.

Sources: NYC OPEN DATA
